Technical Methodology

A multi-stage data pipeline approach was used to ensure data integrity and model robustness.

01. Ingestion

Data Collection

Raw daily AQI data (2015-2020) aggregated from Central Pollution Control Board (CPCB) for Tier-1 cities.

02. Preprocessing

Cleaning & ETL

Handling missing values using KNN imputation. Resampling time-series data to daily frequency.

03. Analysis

Statistical Test

Dickey-Fuller tests for stationarity checks. Seasonal decomposition to isolate trend and seasonality.

04. Modeling

SARIMA & LSTM

Training Seasonal ARIMA for linear trends and Long Short-Term Memory networks for complex non-linear patterns.

Key Observation: The "Delhi Anomaly"

Delhi typically averages an AQI of 200+ (Poor/Severe). During the lockdown phase (March 25 - April 14), daily averages dropped below 60 (Satisfactory) for consecutive days. This provides empirical evidence that vehicular and construction emissions are the primary drivers of the capital's pollution, rather than stubble burning during this specific season.

Predictive Modeling

Using LSTM neural networks trained on 5 years of historical data, we forecasted the post-unlock trends. The model predicted a rapid "V-shaped" recovery in pollution levels as restrictions eased.
